Let $n \in \N$, and let $a, b, c \in \Z$.

(a) $a \equiv a (\mod n)$[^1]
(b) If $a \equiv b (\mod n)$ then $b \equiv a (\mod n)$.
(c) If $a \equiv b (\mod n)$ and $b \equiv c (\mod n)$, then $a \equiv c (\mod n)$.
